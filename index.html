Research Highlights
Internal Variation in Commuting Flows to Employment Centers: Difference by the Skill Level of Workers

  • Did commuters who used to work in the same employment center equally reduce their commutes during the COVID-19 pandemic?

  • We employed multilevel models to analyze the longitudinal changes in commuting flows (from mobile-based mobility data).

  • We demonstrated internal variations in which commuters from more educated residential areas experienced a more rapid decline, primarily due to the ease of adopting remote work among skilled workers.

  • This study is currently under review.

Disparity in Destination Choices Among Elderly Travelers in Seoul

  • Which group of elderly individuals more readily changed their destination choices during the COVID-19 pandemic?

  • We utilized cosine-similarity measures to compare the Origin-Destination (O-D) matrices (from smart-card data) before and after the COVID-19 pandemic.

  • We suggested that elderly individuals with higher socioeconomic status and living closer to the urban center changed their destination choices to a greater extent.

  • Published in Journal of the Korean Regional Science Association with Prof. Jungyul Sohn.
Addressing the Cluttering Problem in Mobility Flow Visualization

  • How can we address the cluttering problem when visualizing mobility data at high spatial resolution?

  • We improved the visualization of human mobility flows by applying the community detection method with a spatial-contiguity constraint.

  • Published in Journal of the Korean Regional Science Association with Prof. Gunhak Lee.
