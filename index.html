Jaegeon Lee

Hi, I am Jaegeon Lee, currently a research team administrator in the Department of Geography at Seoul National University. My research interest includes the travel patterns of elders and commuters, the travel disparity between socioeconomic classes, and the interaction between human mobility and urban forms. In my master's thesis, I researched how unevenly commuting patterns have changed during the COVID-19 pandemic period, advised by Prof. Jungyul Sohn.

I am currently interested in the representation of urban forms from a bottom-up perspective, which can serve as the basis of explaining human mobility flows at the disaggregated level.

Master's Thesis
Differences in Commuting Inflow Patterns among the Employment Districts in Seoul along with the Adoption of Work-from-Home during the COVID-19 Pandemic Period
Jaegeon Lee

Previous studies on the shifting patterns of commutes during the COVID-19 pandemic have primarily focused on the volume of inflows to employment centers, leaving the composition of the inflow population at disaggregated levels relatively unknown. To fill this gap, I examined how the reduction in commuting volume differed across the education level of origin, using Seoul Mobility Data and a longitudinal multilevel model in my thesis. In a case study of Seoul, I found that the employment centers experienced internal differences in which the inflows of highly educated workers decreased at greater rates, as those workers were more feasible to implement remote work. Also, the durability and extent of the internal difference by education level depend on the geographical and industrial characteristics of employment centers. Among the three major CBDs in Seoul, the CBDs specialized in the Financial industry and distant from the highly educated residential areas(Yeoui and Jongno CBDs) were more likely to experience larger internal differences than the other(Gangnam CBD).
